Sinigang

Description

Sinigang is a traditional Filipino sour soup known for its distinctive tangy flavor. It is typically made with a tamarind-based broth and features a variety of ingredients such as meat, vegetables, and sometimes seafood.

Sinigang is often considered a comfort food in Filipino cuisine and is enjoyed throughout the year.

Ingredients

  • Tamarind: 1/2 to 1 cup, fresh tamarind or tamarind paste can be used.
  • Meat: Choose from pork, beef, shrimp, or a combination. Approximately 1 pound, cut into serving pieces.
  • Water: 8 to 10 cups, for the broth.
  • Vegetables:
    • Radish (Labanos): 1 medium-sized, sliced.
    • String Beans (Sitaw): 1 bunch, cut into 2-inch pieces.
    • Eggplant (Talong): 1 large, sliced
    • Leafy Greens (Kangkong or Spinach) 1 bunch, leaves separated.
    • Green Chili Peppers (optional): 2-3 pieces, for added heat.
  • Onion: 1 medium-sized, sliced.
  • Garlic: 4-5 cloves, minced.
  • Fish Sauce (Patis): 2-3 tablespoons, or to taste.
  • Salt and Pepper: To taste.
  • Oil: 1-2 tablespoons, for sautéing.

Steps

  1. Prepare Tamarind Broth:
    • If using fresh tamarind, extract the juice by soaking it in warm water and then straining. If using tamarind paste, dissolve it in water. Set aside the tamarind broth.
  2. Sauté Aromatics:
    • In a pot, sauté garlic and onions in oil until aromatic.
  3. Add Meat:
    • Add the meat pieces and cook until lightly browned.
  4. Pour Tamarind Broth:
    • Pour the tamarind broth into the pot, and bring it to a boil.
  5. Season with Fish Sauce:
    • Add fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Adjust according to preference.
  6. Simmer Meat:
    • Allow the meat to simmer until it becomes tender. This may take around 30 minutes to an hour depending on the meat used.
  7. Add Vegetables:
    • Add the radish, string beans, and eggplant. Simmer until the vegetables are cooked.
  8. Add Leafy Greens and Chili Peppers:
    • Add the leafy greens and green chili peppers (if using). Simmer until the greens are wilted.
  9. Adjust Seasoning:
    • Taste the sinigang and adjust the seasoning if needed. You can add more fish sauce, salt, or pepper according to your taste.
  10. Serve:
    • Serve the sinigang hot with steamed rice. Enjoy the comforting and sour flavors of this Filipino soup!
