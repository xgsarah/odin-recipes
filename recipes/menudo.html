Menudo

Description

Menudo is a traditional Filipino stew that features pork meat, liver, and various vegetables simmered in a rich tomato-based sauce. It is a hearty and flavorful dish often served during special occasions and gatherings.

Menudo is a delicious and comforting Filipino dish that reflects the country's culinary richness. Adjustments can be made to suit personal preferences. Enjoy!

Ingredients

  • Pork (cubed)
  • Pork liver (sliced)
  • Potatoes (cubed)
  • Carrots (cubed)
  • Bell peppers (sliced)
  • Green peas
  • Tomato sauce
  • Tomato paste
  • Onions (chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Fish sauce (patis)
  • Liver spread (optional)
  • Raisins (optional)
  • Bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil
  • Water or pork broth

Steps

  1. Sauté Aromatics:
    • Heat cooking oil in a pot. Sauté chopped onions and minced garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add Pork and Liver:
    • Add the cubed pork and sliced pork liver. Cook until the meat is browned.
  3. Incorporate Tomato Sauce and Paste:
    • Pour in tomato sauce and tomato paste. Mix well and let it simmer for a few minutes.
  4. Pour Water or Broth:
    • Add water or pork broth to the pot. This will be the base of your stew.
  5. Seasoning:
    • Season with fish sauce, salt, pepper, and add bay leaves for flavor. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste.
  6. Add Vegetables:
    • Put in the cubed potatoes and carrots. Allow them to cook until slightly tender.
  7. Include Bell Peppers and Green Peas:
    • Add sliced bell peppers and green peas to the stew. Continue simmering until all vegetables are cooked.
  8. Optional Ingredients:
    • If using liver spread or raisins for added richness and sweetness, add them at this stage.
  9. Final Adjustments:
    • Taste and make final adjustments to the seasoning. You can add more fish sauce, salt, or pepper if needed.
  10. Simmer Until Done:
    • Let the menudo simmer until the pork is tender, and the flavors have melded together. Ensure that the sauce has thickened to your liking.
  11. Serve:
    • Serve the menudo hot over steamed rice. It's often enjoyed with a side of bread or a slice of lemon for added zest.
