Ginisang Mongo

Description

Ginisang Monggo is a nutritious Filipino dish made with mung beans and various aromatics. It is often enjoyed for its comforting and earthy flavors. Adjust the recipe based on your preferences, and feel free to customize it with additional ingredients if desired.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried mung beans (monggo)
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/2 cup pork, sliced into small pieces (optional)
  • 1/2 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined (optional)
  • 1 bunch spinach or malunggay leaves, washed and trimmed
  • Fish sauce or salt, to taste
  • Ground black pepper, to taste
  • 4 cups water (for boiling)

Steps

  1. Prepare Mung Beans:
    • Rinse the dried mung beans thoroughly. In a pot, combine the mung beans and 4 cups of water. Bring to a boil and then reduce heat to simmer until the beans are soft. This usually takes about 30-40 minutes.
  2. Sauté Aromatics:
    • In a separate pan, heat cooking oil. Sauté onions until translucent, then add minced garlic and cook until fragrant. Add diced tomatoes and cook until softened.
  3. Add Meat (Optional):
    • If using pork or shrimp, add them to the pan and cook until the meat is browned (for pork) or until the shrimp turns pink.
  4. Combine with Mung Beans:
    • Once the mung beans are soft, combine them with the sautéed mixture in the pan. Mix well.
  5. Season:
    • Season the mixture with fish sauce or salt and ground black pepper according to your taste preference. Stir to incorporate the flavors.
  6. Add Spinach or Malunggay:
    • Add spinach or malunggay leaves to the mixture. Allow them to wilt in the heat, stirring gently.
  7. Adjust Consistency:
    • Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed. The dish can be soupy or more on the dry side, depending on your preference.
  8. Simmer:
    • Allow the entire mixture to simmer for a few more minutes to let the flavors meld together.
  9. Serve:
    • Serve the Ginisang Monggo hot, preferably over steamed rice.
