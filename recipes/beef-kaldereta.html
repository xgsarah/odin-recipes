Beef Kaldereta

Description

Kaldereta is a Filipino stew that is known for its rich and savory flavor. It traditionally features meat (commonly beef or goat) cooked in a tomato-based sauce with liver paste, vegetables, and sometimes coconut milk. The dish is hearty and often enjoyed with rice.

Kaldereta is a festive and flavorful dish commonly served during special occasions and celebrations in Filipino households. It showcases the country's love for hearty stews and bold flavors.

Ingredients

  • Beef: 1.5 to 2 pounds, cut into stew-sized chunks
  • Tomato Sauce: 1 can (about 15 ounces)
  • Liver Spread or Liver Paste: 2-3 tablespoons
  • Potatoes: 2 to 3 medium-sized, peeled and cut into chunks
  • Carrots: 1 to 2 medium-sized, peeled and sliced
  • Bell Peppers: 2 pieces, preferably red and green, sliced into strips
  • Green Peas: 1/2 cup (optional)
  • Onions: 1 large, finely chopped
  • Garlic: 4 to 5 cloves, minced
  • Cheese: 1/4 cup, grated (optional)
  • Soy Sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Fish Sauce (Patis): 1 to 2 tablespoons
  • Bay Leaves: 2 to 3 pieces
  • Vegetable Oil: 2 to 3 tablespoons
  • Water or Beef Broth: 2 cups
  • Salt and Pepper: to taste
  • Chili Peppers: 1 to 2 pieces, chopped (optional, for added heat)

Steps

  1. Sautéing: In a large pot, heat oil and sauté onions and garlic until aromatic.
  2. Browning the Beef: Add the beef chunks and brown on all sides.
  3. Adding Tomato Sauce: Pour in the tomato sauce and stir well. Let it simmer for a few minutes.
  4. Incorporating Liver Spread: Add the liver spread or liver paste and continue stirring until well combined.
  5. Simmering with Vegetables: Pour water or beef broth into the pot. Add soy sauce, fish sauce, bay leaves, and grated cheese (if using). Bring to a boil and then reduce the heat to simmer. Add potatoes, carrots, bell peppers, and green peas. Allow the mixture to simmer until the meat is tender and the vegetables are cooked.
  6. Seasoning: Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust soy sauce or fish sauce as needed.
  7. Optional: Adding Chili Peppers: If you like it spicy, you can add chopped chili peppers for some heat.
  8. Final Simmer: Let the kaldereta simmer until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency.
