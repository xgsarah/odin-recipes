Chop Suey

Description

Chop Suey is a stir-fried dish that originated in Chinese cuisine but has been adapted and popularized in various forms in different parts of the world, including the Philippines and the United States. The dish typically consists of a mix of meat (often chicken, pork, or beef), various vegetables, and sometimes tofu, all stir-fried together in a flavorful sauce.

Chop Suey is a flexible dish, and the ingredients can be adjusted based on personal preferences. It is often served with rice, making it a popular and satisfying meal. The exact recipe and ingredients can vary regionally and based on individual preferences.

Ingredients

  • Meat: Chicken, pork, or beef, thinly sliced or cubed.
  • Vegetables: A variety of vegetables are used, and the selection can vary. Common choices include:
    • Cabbage
    • Carrots
    • Bell Peppers
    • Snow Peas
    • Baby country
    • Water Chestnuts
    • Mushrooms
    • Bean Sprouts
    • Bok Choy
  • Tofu (optional): Some variations include tofu cubes for added protein.
  • Soy Sauce: A fundamental ingredient in the sauce, providing a savory and salty flavor.
  • Oyster Sauce: Adds a rich and umami flavor to the dish.
  • Cornstarch: Used as a thickening agent for the sauce.
  • Garlic and Ginger: Commonly used for flavoring.
  • Sesame Oil: Often added at the end for a fragrant finish.

Steps

  1. Preparation: Meat and vegetables are usually prepped by slicing or chopping into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Stir-Frying: The meat is stir-fried in a wok or large skillet until cooked. Vegetables are then added and stir-fried until they are cooked but still crisp.
  3. Sauce: A sauce made from soy sauce, oyster sauce, garlic, and ginger is poured over the stir-fried meat and vegetables. Cornstarch may be added to thicken the sauce.
  4. Tofu (if used): If tofu is part of the dish, it can be added and gently stirred into the mixture.
  5. Finishing Touch: Sesame oil is often drizzled over the top for a final touch of flavor.
