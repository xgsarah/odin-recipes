Kare-Kare

Description

Kare-Kare is a traditional Filipino stew known for its rich and savory peanut sauce. It often features oxtail, tripes (beef or pork), and various vegetables.

Kare-kare is often enjoyed with bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) and is typically served with plain or bagoong rice. It is a flavorful and unique dish that showcases the richness of Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Oxtail and/or tripes (beef or pork)
  • Banana hearts (or banana blossoms)
  • String beans
  • Eggplant
  • Peanut butter
  • Annatto seeds (achuete) for coloring (optional)
  • Ground toasted rice
  • Crushed peanuts
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • Oil for sautéing
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Boil the Meat:
    • In a large pot, boil the oxtail and/or tripes until tender. Skim off any impurities that rise to the surface. Reserve the broth.
  2. Prepare Vegetables:
    • While the meat is boiling, prepare the vegetables. Slice the banana hearts, string beans, and eggplant into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Make the Peanut Sauce:
    • In a separate pan, sauté finely chopped onions and garlic in oil until fragrant. Add peanut butter, ground toasted rice, and crushed peanuts. Stir well.
  4. Add Annatto Seeds (Optional):
    • If desired, add annatto seeds soaked in water to the mixture to achieve a reddish color. Discard the seeds after extracting the color.
  5. Combine with Broth:
    • Gradually add the broth from the boiled meat to the peanut mixture, stirring continuously to create a smooth sauce. Adjust the thickness according to preference.
  6. Cook Vegetables:
    • Add the sliced vegetables to the pot with the boiling meat. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Combine Sauce and Meat/Vegetables:
    • Once the meat and vegetables are cooked, combine them with the peanut sauce. Simmer until the flavors meld together.
  8. Season and Serve:
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve kare-kare hot with shrimp paste (bagoong) on the side.
